## What Would Constitute a "Forcing Theorem"?

**Strong forcing theorem** (ideal):
- **Axioms**: 3FLL + distinguishability (with minimal additional structure)
- **Conclusion**: ‚ÑÇ‚Ñô‚Åø is the **unique** mathematical structure consistent with axioms
- **Proof strategy**: Rule out all alternatives (‚Ñù, ‚Ñç, exotic algebras)

**Weak forcing theorem** (acceptable):
- **Axioms**: 3FLL + distinguishability + additional reasonable physical constraints
- **Conclusion**: ‚ÑÇ‚Ñô‚Åø is the "most natural" or "canonical" structure
- **Proof strategy**: Show ‚ÑÇ‚Ñô‚Åø has uniquely desirable properties, alternatives have pathologies

**Negative result** (must confront honestly):
- **Conclusion**: Multiple structures (‚ÑÇ‚Ñô‚Åø, ‚Ñù‚Ñô‚Åø, ‚Ñç‚Ñô‚Åø, etc.) are equally consistent
- **Implication**: ‚ÑÇ‚Ñô‚Åø is a phenomenological choice, not derived necessity
- **Honest response**: Document alternatives, argue why ‚ÑÇ‚Ñô‚Åø is preferred empirically
