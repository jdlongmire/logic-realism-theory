## 2. Key Concepts from Archive

### 2.1 The Bit as 3FLL-Constituted

From Paper 2:
> "The bit is the minimal unit of distinguishability—one binary distinction. In LRT terms:
> - One bit = the information required to distinguish between two alternatives
> - The bit is not derived from physics; physics is derived from bits
> - The grain of actuality is the bit—you cannot have half a distinction"

### 2.2 Wheeler Grounding

> "It from bit; bit from fit."
>
> - "It" (physical reality) emerges from "bit" (binary distinctions)
> - "Bit" emerges from "fit" (3FLL-constituted distinguishability)
> - LRT grounds Wheeler's insight: the yes/no question is the fundamental logical operation

### 2.3 The Bit Scale

> "If the bit is the fundamental unit of distinguishability, and physical action is measured in units of ℏ, then ℏ may be understood as the conversion factor between logical and physical structure."

This connects to S = ℏC (already in Section 22.1).

### 2.4 Pairwise Character

> "To distinguish is to compare. Distinguishability is inherently a relation between two things: how different is A from B? This relational character means distinguishability has quadratic structure—it involves pairs, not individuals."

This explains why quantum mechanics has inner product structure (quadratic, not linear).
