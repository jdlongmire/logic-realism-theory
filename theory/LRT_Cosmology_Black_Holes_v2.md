## Abstract

This paper extends Logic Realism Theory (LRT) to strong-gravity regimes, developing a two-channel model of black hole information processing. Within LRT—where the three classical logical laws ($L_3$: Identity, Non-Contradiction, Excluded Middle) are ontological constraints on instantiation—singularities cannot destroy information but must transform it. We argue that this transformation decomposes infalling configurations into fundamental logical structure, producing two categorically distinct outputs: (1) Hawking radiation remaining instantiated in $A_\Omega$, and (2) ontological return to $I_\infty$, where information ceases instantiation but remains representable.

This two-channel structure generates a sharp, falsifiable prediction: the total mass-energy emitted as Hawking radiation over a black hole's complete evaporation is strictly less than the total mass-energy absorbed ($M_{rad} < M_{in}$). The deficit represents information exiting via the ontological channel. Because $A_\Omega$ is not closed under this dynamics, thermodynamic equilibrium is unattainable—the universe cannot reach heat death.

We distinguish what LRT derives (the existence of two channels, the impossibility of heat death) from what remains empirically open (the branching ratio, the magnitude of the mass-energy deficit). The framework generates a progressive research program with testable predictions distinguishing it from standard cosmology.

**Keywords:** black holes, information paradox, Hawking radiation, cosmology, heat death, Logic Realism Theory, $L_3$, singularity

## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Motivation

The position paper *Logic Realism Theory: Physical Foundations from Logical Constraints* establishes that the three classical logical laws (Identity, Non-Contradiction, Excluded Middle) function as ontological constraints on physical instantiation. This creates a partition between $I_\infty$ (all representable configurations, including contradictions) and $A_\Omega$ (the subset admissible under $L_3$ that can be physically instantiated as stable records).

The GR Extension paper argues that these constraints have implications for spacetime structure: temporal ordering emerges from joint inadmissibility, Lorentzian signature is forced by admissibility asymmetry, closed timelike curves are excluded, and—crucially for this paper—singularities cannot destroy identity-constituting information (Theorem 4).

This paper asks: What do these constraints imply for black hole physics and cosmology?

### 1.2 The Information Paradox in LRT Framing

The black hole information paradox arises from tension between two principles:

1. **Unitarity**: Quantum evolution preserves information; pure states evolve to pure states
2. **Hawking's calculation**: Black hole evaporation produces thermal radiation, apparently destroying information

Modern resolutions (island formula, AdS/CFT) argue that information is preserved—encoded in subtle correlations in Hawking radiation. LRT agrees that information must be preserved but provides a different grounding: $L_3$-admissibility requires that all dynamics preserve identity-constituting structure. A black hole cannot produce $L_3$-violating outputs.

However, LRT adds something these approaches lack: the $I_\infty$/$A_\Omega$ distinction opens a second channel. Information need not exit entirely as radiation. Some may return to $I_\infty$—ceasing instantiation while remaining representable.

### 1.3 Central Claims

This paper argues:

1. **Singularities are decomposition points**: Where identity strain reaches its bound, composite configurations decompose to fundamental $L_3$-admissible structure
2. **Two output channels exist**: Physical (Hawking radiation, remains in $A_\Omega$) and ontological (returns to $I_\infty$)
3. **The mass-energy deficit is nonzero**: $M_{rad} < M_{in}$, with the deficit representing Channel 2 output
4. **Heat death is impossible**: Because $A_\Omega$ is not closed, thermodynamic equilibrium cannot be reached

## 2. Logic Realism Theory: Framework Recap

### 2.1 The $I_\infty$/$A_\Omega$ Distinction

**$I_\infty$ (Infinite Information Space)**: The totality of representable configurations—everything formally specifiable, including contradictions, impossibilities, and $L_3$ violations. $I_\infty$ carries no ontological commitment; it is the space of representational possibility.

**$A_\Omega$ (Admissible Configurations)**: The subset of $I_\infty$ satisfying the three fundamental logical laws:

- **Determinate Identity (Id)**: Every instantiated configuration is determinately what it is, independent of description
- **Non-Contradiction (NC)**: No instantiated configuration is both $P$ and $\neg P$ in the same respect
- **Excluded Middle (EM)**: Every applicable property admits a determinate yes-or-no answer

Only $A_\Omega$ configurations can be physically instantiated as stable records.

### 2.2 It from Bit, Bit from Fit

The LRT hierarchy:

$$\text{Fit} \to \text{Bit} \to \text{It}$$

- **Fit**: $L_3$ admissibility (logical constraint)
- **Bit**: Information structure (representational encoding)
- **It**: Physical instantiation (actual structure in $A_\Omega$)

Physical reality emerges from information, which emerges from logical admissibility.

### 2.3 One-World Realism

LRT maintains one-world realism: there is a single instantiated history in $A_\Omega$. Branching structure exists in $I_\infty$ (the space of possibilities), but only one $L_3$-admissible path is ever actualized. This distinguishes LRT from Many-Worlds interpretations while preserving the mathematical structure of quantum mechanics.

### 2.4 Key Results from Companion Papers

From the GR Extension:

- **Theorem 4 (Identity Preservation)**: No physical process can destroy identity-constituting information. Singularities must be transformations, not endpoints.
- **Identity strain**: The measure of how close configurations are to violating bounded distinguishability. At $\varepsilon_{max}$, identity preservation requires transformation.

From the Born Rule paper:

- **Vehicle-invariance**: Probability assignments cannot depend on mathematically equivalent decompositions
- **Gleason's theorem application**: The Born rule is the unique measure satisfying $L_3$-derived constraints

## 3. Singularity as Decomposition Operator

### 3.1 The Problem of Singularities

General relativity predicts singularities: regions where curvature diverges and geodesics terminate. Standard physics faces a puzzle—what happens to information that falls into a singularity?

Three options:

1. **Destruction**: Information ceases to exist (violates unitarity and $L_3$)
2. **Encoding**: Information is encoded at the horizon or in radiation (standard modern view)
3. **Transformation**: Information is transformed into a different form

LRT requires option 3. By Theorem 4 of the GR Extension, identity-constituting information cannot be destroyed. But what kind of transformation?

### 3.2 Identity Strain at the Singularity

The GR Extension introduces identity strain $\varepsilon$—a measure of how close configurations are to violating bounded distinguishability. The constraint $\varepsilon \leq \varepsilon_{max}$ ensures identity preservation through evolution.

At a singularity, spacetime curvature diverges. In LRT terms, identity strain approaches its bound:

$$\lim_{r \to 0} \varepsilon(r) \to \varepsilon_{max}$$

At this bound, composite configurations cannot maintain their structure. Identity preservation requires decomposition to more fundamental form.

### 3.3 Decomposition to Fundamental Structure

**Definition (Singularity Decomposition)**: At the singularity, where $\varepsilon = \varepsilon_{max}$, composite configurations undergo forced decomposition to fundamental $L_3$-admissible structure—the minimal configurations satisfying Identity, Non-Contradiction, and Excluded Middle.

This is not destruction. The information content is preserved but transformed from composite structure to primitive logical form. Analogously: a molecule dissociating into atoms preserves the constituent matter while transforming its organization.

What is "fundamental structure"? The minimal $L_3$-admissible configuration—a single bit of distinguishability. This connects to the "Bit from Fit" level: at the singularity, configurations are reduced to their logical primitives.

### 3.4 The Mechanism

The singularity functions as a logical "reset point":

1. Composite configuration (electron, photon, complex state) falls in
2. Identity strain increases as $r \to 0$
3. At $\varepsilon_{max}$, composite structure cannot be maintained
4. Configuration decomposes to fundamental bits
5. Fundamental bits exit via available channels

The singularity is not a point of annihilation but a point of maximal simplification—the reduction of complex instantiated structure to primitive logical form.

## 4. The Two-Channel Model

### 4.1 Categorical Distinction

The decomposed fundamental structure has two possible destinations:

**Channel 1: $A_\Omega^{rad}$ (Physical Output)**
- Remains instantiated in $A_\Omega$
- Exits as Hawking radiation
- Subject to thermodynamic entropy accounting
- Observable (in principle) from outside the black hole

**Channel 2: $I_\infty^{ret}$ (Ontological Return)**
- Ceases instantiation
- Returns to representable status in $I_\infty$
- Not subject to $A_\Omega$ entropy accounting
- Not directly observable from within $A_\Omega$

**Critical point**: These are not two descriptions of the same process. They are categorically distinct destinations with different ontological status.

### 4.2 Formal Definition

**Definition (Two-Channel Decomposition Operator)**:

$$D_{sing}: A_\Omega^{in} \to A_\Omega^{rad} \oplus I_\infty^{ret}$$

Where:
- $A_\Omega^{in}$ = infalling configurations (instantiated, $L_3$-admissible)
- $A_\Omega^{rad}$ = Hawking radiation output (remains instantiated)
- $I_\infty^{ret}$ = ontological return (ceases instantiation)

The operator satisfies:

1. **$L_3$-preservation**: All outputs are $L_3$-admissible (for $A_\Omega^{rad}$) or $L_3$-specifiable (for $I_\infty^{ret}$)
2. **Information conservation**: Total information content is preserved across the decomposition
3. **Determinacy**: Each fundamental bit exits via exactly one channel, not both

### 4.3 The Branching Ratio

Define the branching ratio:

$$R = \frac{M_{rad}}{M_{in}}$$

Where:
- $M_{in}$ = total mass-energy of infalling configurations
- $M_{rad}$ = total mass-energy emitted as Hawking radiation

The mass-energy deficit is:

$$\Delta M = M_{in} - M_{rad} = M_{in}(1 - R)$$

**What LRT establishes**: $R < 1$ (two channels exist, so some output goes to Channel 2)

**What LRT does not establish**: The value of $R$

The branching ratio is a physical quantity that must be determined empirically—by measuring Hawking radiation and comparing to infalling mass-energy.

### 4.4 Why Two Channels?

Why must both channels exist? Why not all radiation (Channel 1 only) or all ontological return (Channel 2 only)?

**Channel 1 must exist**: Hawking radiation is an established prediction of quantum field theory in curved spacetime. The LRT framework does not override this; it contextualizes it.

**Channel 2 must exist**: The singularity decomposes configurations to fundamental structure. This fundamental structure exists at the boundary between $A_\Omega$ and $I_\infty$—the minimal $L_3$-admissible form. Some portion returns to the "source" ($I_\infty$) from which instantiation originally drew.

The two-channel structure reflects the fundamental architecture of LRT: physical reality ($A_\Omega$) is a subset of representational possibility ($I_\infty$). At the singularity, the boundary is crossed in both directions.

### 4.5 Comparison Table

| Property | Channel 1: $A_\Omega^{rad}$ | Channel 2: $I_\infty^{ret}$ |
|----------|----------------------------|----------------------------|
| Ontological status | Instantiated | Representable (not instantiated) |
| Physical form | Hawking radiation | None (ceased physical existence) |
| Entropy accounting | Contributes to $A_\Omega$ entropy | Exits entropy accounting |
| Observability | Observable as radiation | Not directly observable |
| Information status | Scrambled but in principle recoverable | Preserved in $I_\infty$, not recoverable from $A_\Omega$ |
| Causal role | Can affect future $A_\Omega$ states | Cannot affect $A_\Omega$ (no longer instantiated) |

## 5. Cosmological Implications

### 5.1 $A_\Omega$ Is Not Closed

The central cosmological implication: if Channel 2 exists, then $A_\Omega$ is not a closed system. Information continuously exits $A_\Omega$ via black hole singularities, returning to $I_\infty$.

This is not information destruction—the information remains representable. But it is information *exit* from the instantiated domain.

### 5.2 Heat Death Requires Closure

The heat death scenario depends on the universe being a closed thermodynamic system:

1. Closed system + second law → entropy increases monotonically
2. Monotonic increase + finite bound → entropy approaches maximum
3. Maximum entropy = thermodynamic equilibrium = heat death

Standard cosmology assumes closure at the fundamental level. Black holes produce entropy (via Hawking radiation), contributing to the approach to equilibrium.

### 5.3 LRT Prevents Heat Death

LRT breaks premise 1. If $A_\Omega$ is not closed—if information continuously exits to $I_\infty$—then:

1. $A_\Omega$ is not closed (Channel 2 removes information)
2. Second law applies locally but not globally to $A_\Omega$
3. Global $A_\Omega$ entropy need not increase monotonically
4. No approach to maximum entropy
5. No heat death

**Theorem (No Heat Death)**: Given the two-channel singularity decomposition model, $A_\Omega$ cannot reach thermodynamic equilibrium.

*Proof*:
1. Black holes exist and eventually evaporate (empirical, Hawking)
2. Singularities decompose configurations via two channels (derived, §3-4)
3. Channel 2 removes information from $A_\Omega$ (definition)
4. Information removal prevents entropy maximization in $A_\Omega$ (thermodynamic)
5. Therefore equilibrium is unattainable ∎

**Epistemic note**: The *rate* at which Channel 2 prevents equilibration depends on the branching ratio $R$, which is empirical. The *impossibility* of equilibrium follows from the existence of Channel 2, which is derived.

### 5.4 The Universe as Circulation System

The LRT picture suggests a circulation:

$$I_\infty \xrightarrow{\text{instantiation}} A_\Omega \xrightarrow{\text{evolution}} \text{black holes} \xrightarrow{\text{decomposition}} A_\Omega^{rad} \oplus I_\infty^{ret}$$

The universe continuously draws from $I_\infty$ (new instantiations) and returns to $I_\infty$ (via Channel 2). This circulation prevents stagnation—the system remains dynamically open.

### 5.5 Open-Ended Evolution

The universe does not approach a final state. It exhibits open-ended evolution:

- New configurations are instantiated from $I_\infty$
- Existing configurations evolve within $A_\Omega$
- Black holes decompose configurations, returning some to $I_\infty$
- The cycle continues indefinitely

This is not chaotic or structureless. $L_3$ constraints ensure all instantiated configurations are admissible. The universe remains logically ordered while avoiding thermodynamic death.

## 6. Testable Predictions

### 6.1 Core Prediction: Mass-Energy Deficit

**Prediction 1**: The total mass-energy emitted as Hawking radiation over a black hole's complete evaporation is strictly less than the total mass-energy absorbed.

$$M_{rad} < M_{in}$$

$$\Delta M = M_{in} - M_{rad} > 0$$

**Comparison**:
- Standard physics predicts $\Delta M = 0$ (complete evaporation)
- LRT predicts $\Delta M > 0$ (Channel 2 deficit)

**Falsification**: Observation of complete black hole evaporation with $M_{rad} = M_{in}$ within measurement precision falsifies the two-channel model.

**Epistemic note**: LRT predicts the deficit exists but not its magnitude. If $\Delta M$ is below detection threshold, the prediction is technically correct but practically unverifiable with given technology.

### 6.2 Modified Page Curve

**Prediction 2**: The entanglement entropy of Hawking radiation follows a modified Page curve that asymptotes below complete information recovery.

Standard Page curve: entropy rises to maximum, then falls to zero as information is fully recovered in radiation.

LRT Page curve: entropy rises, then falls, but asymptotes to $S_{ret} > 0$, representing information that exited via Channel 2.

$$S_{rad}^{final} = S_{in} - S_{ret}$$

**Observable consequence**: Late-time Hawking radiation correlations show incomplete information recovery.

### 6.3 $L_3$-Structure in Radiation

**Prediction 3**: Hawking radiation exhibits $L_3$-admissible structure—determinate outcomes, non-contradictory correlations, definite statistics.

This distinguishes LRT from naive Hawking radiation (purely thermal, no correlations). Modern approaches (island formula) also predict correlations, but LRT predicts correlations with specific logical form reflecting $L_3$ constraints.

**Falsifier**: Hawking radiation violating $L_3$ at the outcome level falsifies all of LRT, not just this extension.

### 6.4 Entropy Bound Correction

**Prediction 4**: Black hole entropy satisfies a corrected Bekenstein-Hawking formula:

$$S_{BH} \leq \frac{A}{4\ell_p^2} - \Delta S_{L_3}$$

Where $\Delta S_{L_3}$ is a correction from $L_3$-preservation, representing information capacity lost to Channel 2.

**Epistemic status**: Existence of correction derived; magnitude empirical.

### 6.5 No Approach to Equilibrium

**Prediction 5**: Over cosmological timescales, universe entropy shows saturation or fluctuation rather than monotonic approach to maximum.

**Practical limitation**: Relevant timescales ($>10^{60}$ years) exceed any feasible observation. The prediction is in principle testable but not practically accessible.

## 7. Relation to Existing Approaches

### 7.1 Island Formula and AdS/CFT

Modern resolutions of the information paradox use the island formula and AdS/CFT to show information is preserved in Hawking radiation correlations.

**LRT perspective**: These approaches correctly describe Channel 1 ($A_\Omega^{rad}$). They show how information can be preserved within instantiated physics. But they do not recognize Channel 2—the possibility that some information exits $A_\Omega$ entirely.

**Key difference**: Island formula predicts $\Delta M = 0$. LRT predicts $\Delta M > 0$.

**Compatibility**: LRT is compatible with island formula results for the radiation channel. The frameworks address different aspects—island formula describes Channel 1 mechanics; LRT adds Channel 2 ontology.

### 7.2 Firewall Paradox

The firewall paradox asks whether infalling observers encounter high-energy excitations at the horizon if Hawking radiation is entangled with the interior.

**LRT perspective**: The paradox assumes all information must exit via radiation. If Channel 2 exists, the entanglement structure differs—some information exits ontologically, potentially dissolving the firewall tension.

**Status**: Suggestive, not a complete resolution. Requires detailed analysis.

### 7.3 Fuzzball Proposal

The fuzzball proposal replaces the classical black hole interior with a horizon-scale structure encoding microstates.

**LRT perspective**: Fuzzballs are compatible with LRT. They represent one possible realization of $L_3$-preserving geometry. LRT does not specify interior structure—only that whatever structure exists must respect $L_3$.

### 7.4 Standard Heat Death Cosmology

Standard ΛCDM predicts heat death: asymptotic approach to maximum entropy equilibrium.

**LRT perspective**: This prediction assumes $A_\Omega$ closure. Channel 2 breaks closure, preventing equilibrium.

**Key difference**: Black holes are entropy redistributors in LRT, not just entropy producers. Some entropy exits $A_\Omega$ rather than accumulating.

### 7.5 Cyclic and Bounce Cosmologies

Penrose's Conformal Cyclic Cosmology and bounce models also avoid heat death via cyclical evolution or pre-big-bang phases.

**LRT perspective**: These models are contingent—they require specific initial conditions and mechanisms. LRT's no-heat-death result is structural—it follows from the $I_\infty$/$A_\Omega$ architecture regardless of initial conditions.

## 9. Objections and Replies

### 9.1 "Channel 2 Is Unobservable—This Is Unfalsifiable"

**Objection**: Information returning to $I_\infty$ cannot be observed from within $A_\Omega$. This introduces unobservable entities, violating empiricism.

**Reply**: Channel 2 itself is unobservable, but its *consequences* are observable. The mass-energy deficit $\Delta M = M_{in} - M_{rad}$ is measurable (in principle). If $\Delta M = 0$, Channel 2 doesn't exist and LRT is falsified. If $\Delta M > 0$, Channel 2 is confirmed via its observable effects.

This parallels quantum mechanics: wavefunctions aren't directly observable, but their consequences (interference patterns, measurement statistics) are. Unobservable theoretical entities with observable consequences are standard in physics.

### 9.2 "Standard Physics Already Preserves Information"

**Objection**: Island formula and AdS/CFT already resolve the information paradox. Information is preserved in Hawking radiation. What does LRT add?

**Reply**: LRT adds a quantitative prediction that differs from standard approaches. Standard physics predicts $\Delta M = 0$ (all information exits as radiation). LRT predicts $\Delta M > 0$ (some exits ontologically). These are empirically distinguishable.

Additionally, LRT provides different *grounding*—information preservation follows from $L_3$ necessity, not from holographic duality or semiclassical calculation. The frameworks may converge on Channel 1 behavior but diverge on whether Channel 2 exists.

### 9.3 "Heat Death Is Well-Established"

**Objection**: Thermodynamics is well-confirmed. The second law has no known exceptions. Why doubt heat death?

**Reply**: LRT does not violate the second law. Entropy increases locally within $A_\Omega$. What LRT denies is that $A_\Omega$ is closed. The second law applies to closed systems; if $A_\Omega$ is open (via Channel 2), global entropy need not maximize.

This is analogous to Earth's biosphere: entropy increases locally, but energy input from the Sun prevents equilibrium. In LRT, information exit via Channel 2 plays an analogous role—preventing $A_\Omega$ from reaching maximum entropy.

### 9.4 "The Branching Ratio Might Be Negligible"

**Objection**: If $R \approx 1$ (almost all information exits as radiation), Channel 2 contributes negligibly. The prediction becomes practically untestable.

**Reply**: This is a genuine limitation. LRT predicts $\Delta M > 0$ but not its magnitude. If the deficit is below detection threshold, the prediction is correct but unverifiable.

However, this is not unique to LRT. Many physical predictions (proton decay rate, gravitational wave background) may be correct but below current detection capabilities. The prediction remains falsifiable in principle—technology may eventually reach the required precision.

Additionally, theoretical work may constrain $R$. If the branching ratio is derivable from $L_3$ or related constraints, we could predict its magnitude rather than merely its sign.

### 9.5 "You Haven't Derived the Decomposition Mechanism"

**Objection**: The claim that singularities decompose configurations to fundamental structure is asserted, not derived. What's the mechanism?

**Reply**: The mechanism follows from identity strain dynamics (GR Extension). As $\varepsilon \to \varepsilon_{max}$, the bounded distinguishability constraint forces transformation. The only transformation preserving $L_3$ at maximal strain is decomposition to fundamental form—composite structure cannot be maintained.

The detailed dynamics (how quickly, via what intermediate stages) remain open. But the endpoint (decomposition to fundamental $L_3$-admissible structure) is constrained by the framework.

### 9.6 "What Determines Which Channel?"

**Objection**: Why does some information go to Channel 1 and some to Channel 2? What determines the split?

**Reply**: We don't know. The branching ratio is empirically determined, not theoretically derived (at present). This is acknowledged as an open question.

However, the *existence* of both channels is derived. Channel 1 must exist (Hawking radiation is established). Channel 2 must exist (fundamental structure at the $A_\Omega$/$I_\infty$ boundary can exit either way). The split between them is what observation must determine.

## 10. Conclusion

Logic Realism Theory, extended to strong-gravity regimes, implies a two-channel model of black hole information processing:

1. **Channel 1 ($A_\Omega^{rad}$)**: Hawking radiation, remaining instantiated
2. **Channel 2 ($I_\infty^{ret}$)**: Ontological return, ceasing instantiation

This structure is not optional within LRT. Singularities must transform (not destroy) information. The transformation decomposes configurations to fundamental $L_3$-admissible structure. This fundamental structure can exit via either channel.

The central prediction is falsifiable: $M_{rad} < M_{in}$. Total Hawking radiation is less than total infalling mass-energy. The deficit represents Channel 2 output.

The cosmological implication is necessary: because $A_\Omega$ is not closed, heat death is impossible. The universe exhibits open-ended evolution, continuously circulating between instantiation and representability.

What remains empirically open is the branching ratio—how much goes to each channel. This must be determined by observation, not derivation. The framework predicts the deficit's existence but not its magnitude.

The research program is progressive: it generates novel predictions ($\Delta M > 0$, modified Page curve, entropy bound corrections) distinguishing LRT from standard cosmology. If these predictions are confirmed, LRT provides a logical foundation for cosmological dynamics. If falsified, the two-channel model requires revision or abandonment.

The universe, on this picture, is not destined for cold equilibrium. It remains dynamically open—an ongoing instantiation of logical possibility from an infinite representational space.

## Appendix A: Formal Definition of the Two-Channel Operator

**Definition (Black Hole Two-Channel Operator)**: Let $\mathcal{D}(A_\Omega^{in})$ denote the space of density operators on infalling configurations. The black hole two-channel operator is:

$$T_{BH}: \mathcal{D}(A_\Omega^{in}) \to \mathcal{D}(A_\Omega^{rad}) \oplus \mathcal{R}(I_\infty^{ret})$$

Where:
- $\mathcal{D}(A_\Omega^{rad})$ = density operators on radiation Hilbert space
- $\mathcal{R}(I_\infty^{ret})$ = representable configurations in $I_\infty$ (not density operators—these have ceased instantiation)

**Constraints**:

1. **$L_3$-preservation**: For all $\rho \in \mathcal{D}(A_\Omega^{in})$, the radiation output $T_{BH}^{(1)}(\rho) \in \mathcal{D}(A_\Omega^{rad})$ is $L_3$-admissible

2. **Information conservation**: 
$$I(T_{BH}(\rho)) = I(\rho)$$
where $I$ measures total information content across both channels

3. **Trace condition**: 
$$\text{Tr}(T_{BH}^{(1)}(\rho)) = R \cdot \text{Tr}(\rho)$$
where $R$ is the branching ratio (fraction to Channel 1)

4. **Determinacy**: Each primitive component of the decomposed state exits via exactly one channel

**Note**: The operator $T_{BH}$ is not trace-preserving on $A_\Omega$ alone (trace is reduced by factor $R$). It is information-preserving across both channels jointly.

## Appendix B: Derivation of No Heat Death Theorem

**Theorem**: Given LRT with two-channel singularity decomposition, $A_\Omega$ cannot reach thermodynamic equilibrium.

**Proof**:

*Premises*:
- P1: Black holes form from gravitational collapse (empirical)
- P2: Black holes evaporate via Hawking radiation (Hawking 1974)
- P3: Singularities decompose configurations via two channels (§3-4)
- P4: Channel 2 removes information from $A_\Omega$ to $I_\infty$ (definition)
- P5: Thermodynamic equilibrium requires a closed system (thermodynamics)

*Argument*:
1. From P1-P2: Black holes exist and eventually evaporate
2. From P3: During evaporation, singularity decomposes infalling information
3. From P4: Some information exits $A_\Omega$ via Channel 2
4. From (3): $A_\Omega$ is not closed—information continuously exits
5. From P5 + (4): $A_\Omega$ cannot reach thermodynamic equilibrium
6. Therefore: Heat death is impossible ∎

**Corollary**: The rate of equilibration prevention depends on:
- Black hole formation rate
- Branching ratio $R$
- Total information flux through Channel 2

These are empirical quantities, but the impossibility of equilibrium is structural.
