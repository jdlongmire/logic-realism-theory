## 1. Theories Under Comparison

### 1.1 Logic Realism Theory (LRT)
- **Core Thesis**: 3FLL are ontological constraints constitutive of physical reality; QM is the unique stable interface between IIS and Boolean actuality
- **Status**: Active research program with Zenodo publication (2025)
- **Principal**: J.D. Longmire (2025)
- **Key Feature**: Structural fine-tuning thesis, not derivation from logic alone
- **Papers**: [5-paper series](theory/)

### 1.2 Standard Quantum Mechanics (SQM)
- **Core Thesis**: Wavefunction + Born rule + measurement postulates
- **Status**: Established framework (1925-present)
- **Principals**: Heisenberg, Schrödinger, Born, Dirac, von Neumann
- **Key Feature**: Predictively successful, operationally complete

### 1.3 Many-Worlds Interpretation (MWI)
- **Core Thesis**: Universal wavefunction, no collapse, branching structure
- **Status**: Established interpretation (1957-present)
- **Principal**: Hugh Everett III (1957), DeWitt, Deutsch, Wallace
- **Key Feature**: Deterministic, no collapse, ontologically profligate

### 1.4 Bohmian Mechanics (Pilot Wave)
- **Core Thesis**: Particles + guiding wave, deterministic, nonlocal
- **Status**: Established interpretation (1952-present)
- **Principal**: David Bohm (1952), de Broglie (1927)
- **Key Feature**: Deterministic trajectories, hidden variables

### 1.5 QBism (Quantum Bayesianism)
- **Core Thesis**: Quantum states represent agent's beliefs, not objective reality
- **Status**: Active interpretation (2000s-present)
- **Principals**: Fuchs, Caves, Schack
- **Key Feature**: Subjective probabilities, agent-centric

### 1.6 It from Bit / Wheeler Program
- **Core Thesis**: Physical reality derives from information ("it from bit")
- **Status**: Research program (1990s-present)
- **Principals**: Wheeler, Zeilinger, various
- **Key Feature**: Information as foundational (programmatic, not unified)

### 1.7 Reconstruction Programs (Hardy, CDP, Masanes-Müller)
- **Core Thesis**: Derive QM from operational/informational axioms
- **Status**: Active research (2001-present)
- **Principals**: Hardy, Chiribella-D'Ariano-Perinotti, Masanes-Müller
- **Key Feature**: Rigorous derivations of Hilbert space structure

### 3.4 Measurement Problem

| Theory | Solution | Mechanism |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| **LRT** | Category transition (IIS → Boolean actuality) | 3FLL enforcement at interface |
| **SQM** | Collapse postulate | Asserted, not explained |
| **MWI** | No collapse, branching | Decoherence + branch structure |
| **Bohmian** | No collapse, definite positions | Particle always has position |
| **QBism** | Belief update | Agent's epistemic state changes |
| **Reconstruction** | Varies | Depends on approach |

**LRT Position**: Dissolves measurement problem by framing it as category transition, not dynamical collapse. Does NOT specify exact physical criterion for when transition occurs (compatible with decoherence, Penrose, GRW).
